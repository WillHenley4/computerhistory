History of Computers

1. how it all started

In 1937, a scientist by the name of George Stiblitz created the "Model K" Adder, this was just one of his components to his relay based model 1 complex calculator, which he made in 1939. Speaking of the year 1939, a german engineer by the name of Konrad Zuse made the Z2 computers which used the same relays as George Stiblitz's calculator used. In 1941, 2 years after the Z2 computer was invented, Konrad Zuse creates the Z3 computer which is a huge improvement from the Z2 computer because the Z3 computer used 2,300 relays, could perform floating point binary arithmetic, and had a 22-bit word length. Sadly, the Z3 computer was destroyed by a ally boming raid on Berlin in 1943. In the 1960's, Zuse recreated the Z3 computer and put it in a museum in Munich, Germany. in 1948, 3 years after World War 2, 3 researchers from the University of Manchester ,Frederic Williams, Tom Kilburn, and Geoff Toothill, developed the "Baby" which was a Small-Scale Experimental Machine. It was used to test new memory Technology that Williams and Kilburn created before the "Baby". This technology is known as the Williams Tube and it was the first memory technology to have high-speed electronic random access memory for computers. Combining the "Baby" and the Williams Tube, they created the first program which consisted of 17 instructions written by Kilburn. This program was ran on June 21st, 1948. That is how computer technology started.

2. Golden Age

In the early 1960's, the number of computer users increased since computers became more mainstream at the time. so, this led to experiments on timesharing computer systems. Timesharing systems can support 100's of users by sharing the computer with each user, this led to the development of CTSS. Programs created for CTSS included RUNOFF, a text formatting utility. CTSS operated until 1973. Tom Kilburn, a man who pioneered computer memory storage, directed a team at University of Manchester and started on a project called an Atlas Computer. The Atlas Computer used virtual memory to use storage capacity and switch rapidly among multiple programs or users. This was a key requirement for timesharing. In 1964, the CDC 6600 is introduced and it can perform up to 3 million instructions per second. At the time it was the fastest supercomputer in the world. The reason it was so fast was because of it's design. It used 10 small computers which were known as peripheral processing units which were capable of offloading the workload from the central processor.

3. Modern Age

In 1977, one of the first PCs was made by the company Commodore called the Commodore PET. It used a MOS Technologies 6502 microprocessor running at 1 MHZ. This computer was really popular with schools and with people who want a computer at home. Commodore really gave way to the PC Industry and was really popular through the 90's. now it is the year 2000, the beginning to the new century, and computer technology has progressed a lot. In 2003, Apple makes the PowerMac G5. the G5 was the first true 64 bit PC, It revolutionized how PCs look today. In 2007, a big thing happened that would shape the future of computers and phones. The first smartphone was created by Apple, known as the iphone. This was huge and Apple has revolutionized phones. A year after the iphone is released, Apple comes out with another banger, the macbook air. Apple used technology that they used for their other computers and combined them to make the macbook air. Apple really shined in the Modern Age.
